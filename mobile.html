Events

Check out our community events!

Numerous community events are held during the year, such as live music and block parties. Every spring, 'Spring Break in the Burg' and the 'Spring Fling' festival is held downtown in Library Park. On Halloween, 'Boo in the Burg' offers special shopping and food specials for patrons. An annual bicycle race known as 'Tour D'Burg' is also held every summer in the downtown area.

Other Events Include:

  • Miamisburg Starving Artists (Open air arts and crafts show)
  • Christmas Starving Artists (November indoor arts and crafts show)
  • Miamisburg Turkey Trot (Thanksgiving Day 8K Run/Walk)
  • Cruise the Burg (Auto show)
  • Thunderburg (Motorcycle show)

Information About Miamisburg

Located just south of Dayton, Ohio, Miamisburg was incorporated as a city into the United States in 1818. Miamisburg gets its name as it is located along the Miami River. The city is most well-known for its involvement in World War II, where it housed secret operations for the War Department in Mound Laboratories. The work of making plutonium detonators was part of the classified work done in these labs. Miamisburg is also known for the Miamisburg Mound, which is a prehistoric Indian burial mound, believed to be built by the Adena Culture. The mound stands at a whopping 68 feet tall with circumference of 852 feet. You can take the 116 concrete steps to the top and get a breath-taking view of the city. The lively downtown Miamisburg is where all kinds of activities take place, such as concerts, dancing in the streets, the Green Tomato Festival, and so many more! All events are family friendly, as it is a great place for your night out.
