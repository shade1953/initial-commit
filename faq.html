What year was Miamisburg founded?

Miamisburg was founded in 1818 by European Americans. It was originally inhabited because of its location next to the river!

Are there tourist attractions around Miamisburg?

Yes! Miamisburg has the Miamisburg Mound, which is a 68 foot tall Indian burial mound from around 200 BC! There are also many parks and nature centers that you can visit within the city. Miamisburg is also very close to outlet malls, movie theaters, bowling alleys, golf courses, as well as being just a short drive to Downtown Dayton.

What are the top places to eat?

There are many local restaurants to choose from! Such as: Ron's Pizza, Bullwinkle's Top Hat Bistro, The Hamburger Wagon, TJ Chumps, Bennett's, and many more! Check out the link on the right to find even more choices!

How big is Miamisburg?

Miamisburg has a population of approximately 20,000 people with a total land area of over 12 square miles. But no matter where you go, it all feels like home!

How are the education programs in Miamisburg's school district?

The educational programs are some of the best in the area. With 6 elementary schools, a state of the art middle school and high school, Miamisburg is ranked third in the Dayton Area for the best academic rate scores. Miamisburg has won the "Excellent District Award" in 2002, 2003, 2004, 2005, 2006, 2007, 2008, 2010, and 2011. For 2012, the district has been awarded the coveted "Excellent with Distinction" status. Miamisburg High School was recognized as a National Blue Ribbon School of Excellence in 1997.

Are there any notable people who came out of Miamisburg?

Yes! More recently we had one of our football players David Bruton ('05) go to the NFL and played for the Denver Broncos and Washington Redskins. Prior to that we have had three other players go professional in football: Ebby DeWeese, George Kinderdine, and Lou Partlow, who was actually the first official person to score a touchdown in the National Football League!
